Section 1

Days of the week

Monday to Sunday — with a Slavic pattern to help you remember.

Bulgarian days have Slavic roots connected to numbers and religion. понеделник (Monday) comes from "next after Sunday". вторник (Tuesday) = "second day". сряда (Wednesday) = "middle". The pattern helps with memorisation.

💡 Day expressions

днес (dnes) = today · утре (utre) = tomorrow · вчера (vchera) = yesterday · вдругиден (vdrugiден) = the day after tomorrow · В понеделник = On Monday (use в before days).

💡 Seasons

пролет (prolet) = spring · лято (lyato) = summer · есен (esen) = autumn · зима (zima) = winter. Say "in summer" as през лятото (prez lyatoto) — literally "through the summer".

Section 3

Telling the time

Asking and giving the time in Bulgarian.

Asking the time: Колко е часът? (What time is it? — literally "how many is the hour?"). The answer gives the hour number followed by часа (o'clock).

TimeBulgarianRomanised
What time is it?Колко е часът?Kolko e chasat?
It is 1 o'clock.Един часа е.Edin chasa e.
It is 3 o'clock.Три часа е.Tri chasa e.
Half past 2.Два и половина.Dva i polovina.
Quarter past 4.Четири и четвърт.Chetiri i chetvart.
Quarter to 5.Без четвърт пет.Bez chetvart pet.
20 past 3.Три и двадесет.Tri i dvadesset.

🎤 Telling the time

💡 Before and after the hour

For times after the hour: say hour + и + minutes: три и двадесет (3:20). For times before the hour: без (without/minus) + minutes + hour: без десет шест (10 to 6 = 5:50). The 24-hour clock is used in official contexts: четиринадесет и тридесет = 14:30.

Section 5

Dialogue — making plans

Using days, months and time together.

💬 Dialogue — arranging to meetRead aloud

— Искаш ли да се срещнем?
— Да, с удоволствие! Кога?
— В събота? Имам свободно след обяд.
— В колко часа?
— В три и половина. Пред кафенето на улица "Граф Игнатиев".
— Добре. До събота тогава!
— Чао! До скоро.

— Iskash li da se sreshtem?
— Da, s udovolstvie! Koga?
— V sabota? Imam svobodno sled obyad.
— V kolko chasa?
— V tri i polovina. Pred kafeneto na ulitsa Graf Ignatiev.
— Dobre. Do sabota togava!
— Chao! Do skoro.

— Would you like to meet up?
— Yes, with pleasure! When?
— On Saturday? I am free after noon.
— At what time?
— At half past three. In front of the café on Graf Ignatiev Street.
— Good. Until Saturday then!
— Bye! See you soon.
