Gender & the
Definite Article

Bulgarian has no separate word for "the" — it attaches to the end of the noun. Today you learn gender, how to spot it, and exactly which ending to add.

Section 1

The three genders

Every Bulgarian noun is masculine, feminine or neuter — and the ending tells you which.

Unlike French or Spanish, where gender must be memorised for every word, Bulgarian noun gender can usually be predicted from the ending. Learn these three simple rules and you will guess correctly about 85% of the time.

GenderTypical endingExamplesRule reliability
MasculineConsonantмъж (man), ден (day), стол (chair), брат (brother)Very reliable — most consonant-ending nouns are masculine
Feminine-а or -яжена (woman), маса (table), земя (earth), майка (mother)Very reliable — almost all -а/-я nouns are feminine
Neuter-е or -одете (child), кафе (coffee), море (sea), село (village)Very reliable — -е/-о nouns are almost always neuter
🎤 Gender examples

⚠️ Exceptions worth knowing

A few common words break the pattern: баща (father) ends in -а but is masculine. нощ (night) ends in a consonant but is feminine. Learn these common exceptions as you encounter them — there are not many.

Section 2

The definite article — "the"

"The" is a suffix, not a separate word. Four forms, one rule.

This is one of the most distinctive features of Bulgarian. Instead of putting a separate word like "the" before a noun, Bulgarian attaches it to the end. The form depends on the noun's gender and number.

GenderIndefinite (a/an)Definite singular (the)Plural (some)Definite plural (the ... s)
Masculineстол (a chair)столът / стола (the chair)столове (chairs)столовете (the chairs)
Feminineмаса (a table)масата (the table)маси (tables)масите (the tables)
Neuterдете (a child)детето (the child)деца (children)децата (the children)
🎤 Definite article examples

💡 Столът vs стола — two masculine forms

Masculine definite singular has two forms: -ът when the noun is the grammatical subject (Столът е тук — The chair is here), and -а when it is the object (Виждам стола — I see the chair). For now, use -ът as your default — it will be right most of the time.

Section 3

Plurals — how to form them

Three patterns cover almost every noun.

GenderPlural endingExamples
Masculine-ове / -истол → столове (chairs), брат → братя (brothers), лекар → лекари (doctors)
Feminine-имаса → маси (tables), жена → жени (women), книга → книги (books)
Neuter-а / -тадете → деца (children), море → морета (seas), кафе → кафета (coffees)
🎤 Plural forms

💡 The definite plural is always -те

Whatever the gender, the definite plural always ends in -те: столовете, масите, децата. This consistency makes it easy — once you know the plural, just add -те (or use -та for neuter). The -те ending is one of the most reliable patterns in Bulgarian.

Section 5

Dialogue — asking about things

Using the definite article naturally in conversation.

💬 Dialogue — where is the ...?Read aloud

— Извинете, къде е банката?
— Банката е на улица "Витоша", до хотела.
— А аптеката?
— Аптеката е срещу гарата. Не е далеч.
— Благодаря много!
— Няма нищо. Приятен ден!

— Izvinete, kade e bankata?
— Bankata e na ulitsa Vitosha, do hotela.
— A aptekata?
— Aptekata e sreshtu garata. Ne e dalech.
— Blagodarya mnogo!
— Nyama nishto. Priyaten den!

— Excuse me, where is the bank?
— The bank is on Vitosha Street, next to the hotel.
— And the pharmacy?
— The pharmacy is opposite the station. It's not far.
— Thank you very much!
— No problem. Have a nice day!

Section 6

Writing task

✏️ Writing task — Day 6

  1. Write the gender (M/F/N) for: книга, море, лекар, майка, кафе, брат.
  2. Put the definite article on: жена, стол, дете.
  3. Make these plural: маса, книга, стол.
  4. Translate: "The child is in the room. The mother is at the table."
Show answers

1. книга (F), море (N), лекар (M), майка (F), кафе (N), брат (M)

2. жената, столът, детето

3. маси, книги, столове

4. Детето е в стаята. Майката е на масата.

💡 Tip: Writing by hand in Cyrillic is the fastest way to lock in new vocabulary. Even five minutes of handwriting beats reading the same words ten times.
