Section 1

The Qualification Opportunity

There are two internationally recognised Bulgarian language certificates. Reframing your course around them transforms it from "a free course" into "the free path to a real European qualification." That is a much more compelling, shareable proposition.

🎓 STBFL — Standard Test of Bulgarian as a Foreign Language

Run by Sofia University "St. Kliment Ohridski" — the most respected Bulgarian qualification. Accepted across Bulgaria for university admissions, employment, and citizenship applications.

  • Five levels: A2, B1, B2, C1, C2
  • Written exam: 145–175 mins at A2/B1
  • Oral exam: ~20 minutes via Teams
  • Cost: ~€265 per level
  • Conducted at their Sofia premises or online
🇪🇺 ECL — European Certificate of Attainment

A pan-European exam consortium covering A2–C1. Focuses purely on communicative skills — no grammar translation, making it more accessible for practical learners.

  • Four levels: A2, B1, B2, C1
  • Two oral and two written tasks per level
  • A2 writing: two texts of ~50 words
  • B1 writing: two texts of ~100 words
  • Widely recognised across EU member states
🏛️ Bulgarian Citizenship Exam — the Practical Goal for Many Learners

Many of your likely users are British or EU expats moving to Bulgaria, or people applying for Bulgarian citizenship. The state citizenship language exam is a 20-question test, 1 hour, 60% pass mark — essentially an A2 test. Your course, properly structured, could make this the primary CTA: "Pass the Bulgarian citizenship language exam."

The Big Idea: Rename and reframe the course as "The free path to Bulgarian A2/B1 certification." Every module should end with a mini practice test. The homepage should feature a clear learning path: A1 foundations → A2 certificate → B1 certificate → citizenship exam ready.

Section 2

What the Exam Actually Tests

Understanding the exam structure lets us ensure every module directly prepares learners for a real assessment. The four skill areas below should each get their own practice format in the redesigned course.

🎧

Listening

Short audio texts and dialogues. Candidates must understand meaning, take notes, and find specific information. Duration: 40 mins / 55 points at A2.

Your course has: TTS audio buttons but no comprehension questions after listening.

📖

Reading

400–600 word texts at A2, 500–700 at B1. Multiple choice, matching, sentence completion. Everyday topics: family, travel, food, hobbies.

Your course has: Vocabulary tables but no connected reading passages.

✍️

Writing

Two written texts per level. A2: ~50 words each. B1: ~100 words each. Assessed on grammar, vocabulary, coherence, and appropriate register.

Your course has: "Write three short lines" prompts but no structured writing tasks.

🗣️

Speaking (Oral Exam)

4 parts: self-introduction, role-play dialogue, monologue on a topic, commentary on a picture or text. ~20 minutes with an examiner.

Your course has: "Say aloud" instructions but no structured speaking practice scaffolding.

Issues to Fix

Identified problems across design, structure, and content — prioritised by impact on learner experience.

Design

Navigation overload

14 nav items in the header is unusable. Collapses to a plain select on mobile with no styling.

Design

Generic visual identity

System fonts, cold blue/white palette, no warmth or cultural character. Looks like a Bootstrap template.

Design

No visual rhythm in modules

Each module is a single long scroll. Days have no clear visual "chapter" boundary.

Design

Audio buttons easy to miss

Inline blue buttons blend in with surrounding text. No visual "audio zone" signal.

Design

No within-module progress

No indicator showing "Day 3 of 5" within a module page. Learners lose their place.

Design

Module 13 is a different site

The interactive doctor module has completely different styling from all other pages.

Structure

No CEFR framing

No mention of A1/A2/B1 levels anywhere. Learners have no clear progression milestone.

Structure

Module 1 is missing Day 1

Homepage module descriptions too brief

Cards only show a title. No day range, CEFR level, time estimate, or topic tags visible.

Inconsistent module titles

Homepage titles differ from module page titles (e.g. "Food and shopping" vs "Eating Out and Shopping"). Some have emojis, some don't.

No "Contents" TOC that works

The "Contents" section at the top of each module is just a static text list — not anchor-linked or sticky.

No Day 1 proper lesson

Content

Content

No exam-style practice tasks

No reading passages

Content

No writing tasks

Section 6

New Modules & Pages to Add

These new pages fill the gaps the exam reveals, and add features that make the course distinctive.

📖 Module 0: The Alphabet — A Proper Day 0 Lesson

Currently the alphabet is just a table on the homepage. This needs to become a proper first lesson: letter groups, pronunciation audio for every letter, a Cyrillic handwriting guide, and a 10-question "read these letters" quiz. This is what every beginner needs before Day 1.

  • 30 letter cards with audio (TTS)
  • Grouped by similarity to Latin letters
  • Letters that look the same but sound different (e.g. В = V, Н = N)
  • Quick 10-question alphabet recognition quiz

🧪 Practice Exam Pages (A2 + B1)

Two standalone exam simulation pages, each clearly structured around the real STBFL format. Timed. Interactive. With self-marking multiple choice sections and guided writing/speaking prompts with model answers to compare against.

  • Timer visible throughout (optional: can be hidden)
  • Section-by-section structure matching real exam
  • Self-assessment rubric after each speaking/writing task
  • Results summary: strengths and areas to revisit

📚 Glossary Page (all 60 days in one place)

A searchable, filterable word list compiled from all modules. Searchable by English or Bulgarian. Filterable by module / topic. Each entry has an audio button. This becomes the course's reference tool and dramatically improves SEO.

🗺️ "How to Get Certified" Page

A simple guide explaining the STBFL and ECL exams: who they're for, how to register, what to expect on the day, and links to official exam websites. Positions your course as the preparation path. A very low-effort page with high value for the citizenship exam audience.

Section 7

Design Redesign Plan

A warm, literary, culturally-rooted aesthetic — evoking Bulgarian culture, the Cyrillic script's history, and the joy of language learning. Think refined editorial, not clinical e-learning software.

🎨 New Colour Palette
#1a1f4e — Deep Cyrillic Navy (primary, headers, nav)
#fdf8f0 — Warm Cream (page background)
#e8a020 — Bulgarian Gold (accent, CTAs, highlights)
#8b1a2e — Deep Rose (Bulgarian rose motif, badges)
#2a6347 — Forest Green (completed states, success)
🔤 Typography
  • Headings: Playfair Display (elegant, literary, works beautifully for a language course — serif gravitas)
  • Body text: Source Sans 3 (clean, readable, excellent Cyrillic support)
  • Bulgarian script: Displayed larger (110%) than Latin equivalents, slightly bolder — make the Cyrillic the star
  • Day numbers: Large display numerals in navy — create clear visual chapter breaks
  • Code/transliteration: A monospaced font for phonetic guides
🧭 New Navigation Design
  • Homepage nav: Clean horizontal bar with phase groupings: "Foundations (A1)" / "Intermediate (A2)" / "Advanced (B1)" / "Practice Exams" — not 14 individual links
  • Module pages: Sticky left sidebar (desktop) showing the 5 day sections, highlighting current one as you scroll. Collapses to a top progress strip on mobile
  • Mobile: Full-screen slide-in menu with module tiles grouped by CEFR level
  • Breadcrumb: "Home → A2 Phase → Module 4 → Day 17" always visible
  • Previous/Next: Large bottom navigation cards showing the next day/module title
📐 Module Page Layout
  • Day header: Full-width coloured band with "Day 16" in large type, day title, estimated time, and CEFR level badge
  • Audio zones: Visually distinct pale blue cards with speaker icon — not inline buttons
  • Vocabulary tables: Alternating row colours, inline audio per word, phonetic guide column, Bulgarian script enlarged
  • Dialogue sections: Chat-bubble style layout (speaker A left, speaker B right) instead of plain text
  • Grammar explainers: Collapsible "Grammar Note" accordions — visible but not overwhelming
  • Panic button: Floating bottom-right "lifeline" button (stays in view while scrolling)
  • Day complete: A satisfying "Mark Day Complete ✓" button at the bottom of each day section
🏠 Homepage Redesign
  • Hero: Full-width banner — "Learn Bulgarian. Earn a Real European Certificate." with a "Start for free" CTA and a small credential logo strip (STBFL / ECL / CEFR)
  • Progress dashboard: Prominent "You're on Day X of 60" tracker for returning users
  • Learning path visual: A visual roadmap showing A1 → A2 → B1 phases, with modules plotted along it
  • Module cards: Show level badge, day range, key topics as tags, estimated time, and completion status
  • "Why get certified?" section: Short section explaining STBFL/ECL/citizenship exam with a link to the certification guide page
Section 8

Exam Practice Features to Add

These features don't require a backend — they can all be built in vanilla JS within HTML pages, just like the existing TTS and panic button features.

Feature
A
End-of-Day

Mini Practice Test (4 questions)

After each day's vocabulary, a 4-question interactive quiz: 2 multiple-choice word match questions, 1 gap-fill sentence, 1 "which is correct?" grammar choice. Auto-marked. Score shown. Wrong answers explain the correct form.

  • Uses vocabulary from that day only
  • Randomises option order each time
  • Triggers a "Day complete" checkmark on pass (≥3/4)
Feature
B
End-of-Module

Exam-Style Module Test (4 skills)

At the end of each module, a structured 4-part test mirroring the real exam: Listen (TTS passage + 5 questions), Read (short text + 5 questions), Write (prompt + self-check rubric), Speak (prompt + model answer reveal).

  • Listen + Read sections auto-marked
  • Write section provides a model 50-word answer to compare
  • Speak section provides a full model script to read aloud
Feature
C
Milestone

Can-Do Checklist (A1, A2, B1)

At modules 3, 7, and 12, a self-assessment card appears with the official CEFR "can-do" statements for that level. Learners tick off what they can do. A visual percentage bar fills up. This is exactly how real CEFR self-assessment works.

  • A1: 12 can-do statements
  • A2: 18 can-do statements
  • B1: 24 can-do statements
  • Browser saves progress
Feature
D
Standalone

Full Mock Practice Exams (A2 + B1)

Two dedicated exam pages replicating the STBFL structure. Optional countdown timer. Section-by-section navigation. Auto-marked MCQ sections. Writing and speaking sections provide model answers and a self-scoring rubric using the official assessment criteria.

  • A2 exam: 3 hours total (split into sessions if needed)
  • B1 exam: 3 hours total
  • Downloadable self-assessment PDF at the end
