Opinions &
Agreeing / Disagreeing

Real conversation requires expressing what you think, asking what others think, and respectfully pushing back. Today's language unlocks genuine discussion in Bulgarian — essential for the citizenship exam oral component.

📅 Day 34⏳ ~60 minutes🎤 Audio on every section✅ Quiz at the end
🔊 Speed:

🗣 Setting the scene

Modern Bulgarian life runs on opinions. People debate politics, prices, cities, schools, traditions, football, the euro, Sofia traffic, and whether life is better in the capital or in the provinces. If you want to sound natural in Bulgaria, you need more than vocabulary: you need ways to agree, soften disagreement, and ask others what they think without sounding abrupt.

This lesson is where conversation starts to feel real. It teaches not only how to express a view, but how Bulgarians actually negotiate difference in everyday talk.

Section 3

Disagreeing — politely and directly

How to push back without offending.

Disagreeing politely in Bulgarian often starts with acknowledging the other person's point before introducing your own. Direct contradiction is possible but can sound blunt — the softeners below make disagreement sound natural and respectful.

BulgarianRomanisedEnglishStyle
Не съм съгласен / Не съм съгласна Ne sam saglasen/a.I disagree.Direct
Не мисля така Ne mislya taka.I don't think so.Direct
Да, но Da, no...Yes, but...Softened
Разбирам те, но / Разбирам ви, но Razbiram te/vi, no...I understand you, but...Diplomatic
Има и друга гледна точка Ima i druga gledna tochka.There is another point of view.Diplomatic
Не съм напълно убеден / Не съм напълно убедена Ne sam napalno ubeden/a.I'm not entirely convinced.Hedged
Бих поставил под въпрос / Бих поставила под въпрос Bih postavil/a pod vapros...I would question...Formal/careful
Това е малко пресилено Tova e malko presileno.That's a bit of an exaggeration.Challenging
Не е точно така Ne e tochno taka.That's not quite right.Gentle correction
Зависи от гледната точка Zavisi ot glednata tochka.It depends on the perspective.Neutral

Section 6

Dialogue — a real discussion

Two friends debate whether to move to a bigger city.

💬 Dialogue — should we move to Sofia?Read aloud

— Мисля, че трябва да се преместим в София. По-добри възможности за работа.
— Разбирам те, но не съм сигурна. София е много скъпа.
— Да, но заплатите също са по-високи. Освен това животът там е по-интересен.
— Според мен провинцията предлага по-добро качество на живот. По-спокойно е.
— Съгласен съм, но не бих казал, че е скучно в София. Има толкова много да правиш.
— Да, но пък трафикът е ужасен. И апартаментите — много са скъпи.
— Имаш право за апартаментите. Въпреки това смятам, че си струва да опитаме.
— Не съм напълно убедена. Трябва да помислим по-сериозно.
— Разбира се. Но поне можем да проучим възможностите, нали?
— Добре, от друга страна — защо не? Нека поговорим с някои хора там.

— Mislya, che tryabva da se premestim v Sofiya. Po-dobri vazmozhnosti za rabota.
— Razbiram te, no ne sam sigurna. Sofiya e mnogo skaра.
— Da, no zaplatite sashto sa po-visoki. Osven tova zhivotаt tam e po-interesen.
— Spored men provintsiуata predlaga po-dobro kachestvo na zhivot. Po-spokoyno e.
— Saglasen sam, no ne bih kazal, che e skuchno v Sofiya. Ima tolkova mnogo da pravish.
— Da, no pak trafikat e uzhasen. I apartamentite — mnogo sa skapi.
— Imash pravo za apartamentite. Vapreki tova smyatam, che si struva da opiтame.
— Ne sam napalno ubedena. Tryabva da pomislim po-seriozno.
— Razbirase. No poне mozhеm da prouchim vazmozhnostite, nali?
— Dobre, ot druga strana — zashto ne? Neka pogovorim s nyakoi hora tam.

— I think we should move to Sofia. Better work opportunities.
— I understand you, but I'm not sure. Sofia is very expensive.
— Yes, but the salaries are also higher. Besides, life there is more interesting.
— In my opinion the provinces offer a better quality of life. It's more peaceful.
— I agree, but I wouldn't say it's boring in Sofia. There's so much to do.
— Yes, but the traffic is terrible. And the apartments — they are very expensive.
— You're right about the apartments. Nevertheless, I think it's worth trying.
— I'm not entirely convinced. We need to think more seriously.
— Of course. But at least we can explore the options, right?
— OK, on the other hand — why not? Let's talk to some people there.

Section 7

Writing task

✏️ Writing task — Day 34

  1. Write your opinion on one of these topics using at least 5 opinion phrases: (a) learning Bulgarian (b) living in a big city (c) the importance of traditions.
  2. Translate: "In my opinion, Bulgarian culture is fascinating. I am not entirely sure why, but I think it is because of the history."
  3. Rewrite "I don't agree" in three different ways — from direct to diplomatic.
  4. Write a short dialogue where two people disagree politely about whether Bulgaria is a good country to live in.
Show answers

1. Free answer — use мисля, че · според мен · убеден/а съм · струва ми се · защото · освен това.

2. Според мен българската култура е завладяваща. Не съм напълно сигурен/а защо, но мисля, че е заради историята.

3. Direct: Не съм съгласен/а. Moderate: Не мисля така. Diplomatic: Разбирам те, но има и друга гледна точка.

4. Free answer — use Разбирам те, но... · Да, но... · Не съм напълно убеден/а... · Зависи от гледната точка.

💡 Tip: Writing by hand in Cyrillic is the fastest way to lock in new vocabulary. Even five minutes of handwriting beats reading the same words ten times.
