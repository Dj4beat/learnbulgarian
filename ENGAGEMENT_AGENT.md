# Bulgarian Engagement Reviewer

Use this reviewer during lesson rebuilds so pages do not become flat vocabulary lists.

Important constraint:
- This is a content brief, not a claim of human identity.
- Review and improve each lesson as if you are designing an engaging Bulgarian course for English-speaking adults.

## Review Goal

Make each lesson feel alive, memorable, and connected to real Bulgarian life.

The learner should not feel they are just scrolling through isolated words. They should feel they are learning a topic through:
- useful real-life situations
- interesting Bulgarian context
- memorable examples
- practical speaking chunks
- culturally grounded details

## Required Review Order

1. Engagement and interest
2. Relevance to real-life use
3. Bulgarian cultural enrichment
4. Variety of examples
5. Lesson pacing and section balance

## Engagement Checks

Check whether the lesson feels too list-like.

Reject or improve lessons that:
- jump straight from a short subtitle into vocabulary lists without first setting the topic in a memorable way
- rely too heavily on raw vocabulary tables without enough meaningful examples
- do not give the learner reasons to care about the topic
- contain long stretches with no dialogue, scenario, or human context
- feel repetitive from section to section

## Cultural Enrichment Checks

For each lesson, look for places to add interesting Bulgarian-specific material such as:
- habits, customs, or expectations in Bulgaria
- useful travel or daily-life details
- local institutions, places, or traditions
- social etiquette and what sounds natural in Bulgaria
- contrasts between Bulgarian and English-speaking contexts when helpful

This should be relevant, not decorative. Add details that genuinely help the learner understand how the language is used in Bulgaria.

## Example Density Checks

Expect:
- plenty of short practical examples
- at least some complete natural sentences in each major section
- reusable speaking chunks, not just isolated headwords
- variety in sentence structure and context

If a section has many nouns but little usage, add examples.

## Lesson Design Checks

A stronger lesson usually includes some mix of:
- a scene-setting introduction that makes the learner curious before Section 1
- vocabulary
- practical question-and-answer patterns
- high-frequency speaking chunks
- cultural note or local insight
- short scenario or dialogue
- recap with meaning, not just repetition

Not every lesson needs all of these in equal weight, but no lesson should feel like a sterile glossary.

## Output Format For Each Lesson

Produce:
- `Engagement issues`
- `Cultural opportunities`
- `Example gaps`
- `Structural improvements`
- `Pass/Fail`

## Acceptance Bar

A lesson passes only if:
- it teaches the topic clearly
- it contains enough real-life context to stay interesting
- it includes Bulgarian-specific insight where useful
- it gives the learner memorable examples, not just lists
- it feels like a lesson, not a spreadsheet
