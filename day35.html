A2 grammar — complete reference

Every grammar point from Days 16–34 in one place.

Grammar pointKey ruleExample
Aorist (simple past) Group 1: stem + -х/-е/-е/-хме/-хте/-ха. Group 2: stem + -их/-и/-и/-ихме/-ихте/-иха. Key irregulars: бях, отидох, дойдох, взех, казах, видях, ядох, пих.Вчера отидох на пазара. Тя каза нещо важно.
Future tense ще + present tense (all persons). Negative: няма да + present. No new endings.Ще отида утре. Няма да работя в събота.
Comparatives по- + adjective (more). най- + adjective (most). Very regular.по-евтин, по-голям, най-добър, по-бавно
Reflexive verbs се = action on oneself (събуждам се, обличам се). си = for oneself (лягам си, мия си зъбите).Събуждам се в шест. Лягам си в единадесет.
Frequency adverbs винаги · обикновено · често · понякога · рядко · никога (+ не)Никога не закъснявам. Рядко гледам телевизия.
Боли ме construction боли ме + singular body part. болят ме + plural body part.Боли ме главата. Болят ме зъбите.
Profession gender Every Bulgarian profession has masculine and feminine forms. Feminine often adds -ка or -ица.лекар, лекарка, учител, учителка, програмист, програмистка
Работя като Profession after като or съм: bare noun, no definite article.Работя като инженер.
Честит agreement Честит (M) · Честита (F) · Честито (N) — agrees with noun.Честит рожден ден. Честита сватба. Честито бебе.
Seasons + през през + definite season noun: през пролетта, през лятото, през есента, през зимата.През лятото е горещо. През зимата вали сняг.
Object pronouns (intro) го (M/N) · я (F): Ще го взема (M noun). Ще я взема (F noun).Ще го купя. Ще я взема.
Opinion phrases Мисля, че · Според мен · Смятам, че · Убеден съм, че · Струва ми сеСпоред мен е важно. Струва ми се, че греши.

Section 3

Bulgarian citizenship exam — complete guide

Everything you need to know before sitting it.

Naturalisation requirements and test administration can change, so treat this section as orientation, not legal advice. The safest approach is: use this lesson to prepare the language and culture, then confirm the current official process with the Ministry of Justice or the relevant Bulgarian authority handling your application.

AspectDetails
Official aim The test is designed to show practical Bulgarian ability and basic civic-cultural knowledge for naturalisation applicants.
Working level This course prepares you around A2, which is the right zone for the type of language usually associated with entry-level integration and citizenship preparation.
Likely topics Expect grammar, vocabulary, reading, Bulgarian holidays, history, geography, symbols, and well-known national figures.
Course fit Days 1-35 give you the language and cultural base. Day 31 is especially important for national symbols, holidays, and cultural references.
Current procedure Always verify the latest format, booking process, fees, location, and required documents with the official institution handling your case.
Best strategy First make your Bulgarian reliable. Then check the official instructions and practise under timed conditions with mock questions like the ones below.

🔎 Important exam reality

The official sample tests published by the Institute of Education are written entirely in Bulgarian. That means learners should not expect English prompts or English answer choices in the real test. You may still see administrative guidance in Bulgarian on the official site, but the actual sample test content is Bulgarian-only.

🏆 What this course covers vs the exam

This course covers the core language and culture you need for citizenship preparation: everyday vocabulary, essential grammar, basic reading, national holidays, geography, symbols, traditions, and major historical-cultural references. If you can work confidently through the Day 15 and Day 35 mocks, you are in a strong position for the language side of preparation. Official procedures and precise test administration should still be checked separately.

💡 Your B1 reset checklist before Day 36

Before you start Day 36, make sure you can: (1) Conjugate regular verbs in present AND aorist — both groups. (2) Form the future with ще and the negative with няма да. (3) Use боли ме / болят ме correctly. (4) Know all 11 Bulgarian national holidays with dates. (5) State all Bulgarian neighbours and the highest peak. If any of these feel shaky, revisit the relevant day before moving on.
