The Verb
'To Be'

The verb 'to be' (съм) is the most important verb in Bulgarian. It is irregular, so it must be memorised — but it appears in hundreds of sentences you will use every single day.

📅 Day 5⏳ ~60 minutes🎤 Audio on every section✅ Quiz at the end
🔊 Speed:

Section 1

Full conjugation of съм — six forms, all irregular

Every form must be memorised. The good news: these six words unlock hundreds of sentences.

Bulgarian drops the subject pronoun in everyday speech — the verb ending already shows who you mean. You will hear Съм учител (I am a teacher) just as often as Аз съм учител. Both are correct; dropping the pronoun is more natural. Each card below has its own audio — click and repeat aloud.

I — Аз (az) съм sam Аз съм учител. — I am a teacher.
You (inf) — Ти (ti) си si Ти си тук. — You are here.
He — Той (toy) е e Той е лекар. — He is a doctor.
She — Тя (tya) е e Тя е учителка. — She is a teacher.
It — То (to) е e То е хубаво. — It is nice.
We — Ние (nie) сме sme Ние сме готови. — We are ready.
You (form/pl) — Вие (vie) сте ste Вие сте българи. — You are Bulgarians.
They — Те (te) са sa Те са там. — They are there.

💡 He / She / It all use е

Third-person singular always uses е regardless of gender. The pronoun changes (той / тя / то) but the verb stays the same. This is simpler than most European languages where the verb might also change.

💡 Memory trick — the six forms

Group them as: съм · си · е (singular) and сме · сте · са (plural). Notice the plural forms all end in a vowel + consonant pattern. Chant them as a rhythm: sam · si · e — sme · ste · sa until they are automatic.

Section 2

Negative forms — не + verb

Place не directly before the verb. One rule, no exceptions.

To negate any form of съм, place не (ne = not) directly before the verb. The word order and everything else stays the same. No auxiliary verb, no restructuring — simply insert не.

аз съм → аз не съм I am not
ти си → ти не си you are not
той / тя / то е → той / тя / то не е he / she / it is not
ние сме → ние не сме we are not
вие сте → вие не сте you are not (formal)
те са → те не са they are not

💡 не always goes before the verb

Unlike English, where "not" can go in several positions, Bulgarian не always immediately precedes the verb. Тя не е вкъщи ✓   Тя е не вкъщи ✗. This rule applies to every Bulgarian verb, not just съм.

Section 3

Yes/No questions with ли

One tiny particle turns any statement into a question — no restructuring needed.

To turn any Bulgarian statement into a yes/no question, add the particle ли directly after the verb. The focus word — the thing you are asking about — moves to the front of the sentence. This is very different from English, which uses do/does/is/are restructuring.

StatementQuestion with лиEnglishAudio
Той е лекар.Лекар ли е той?Is he a doctor?
Вие сте готови.Готови ли сте вие?Are you ready?
Тя е у дома.У дома ли е тя?Is she at home?
Те са тук.Тук ли са те?Are they here?
Ние сме готови.Готови ли сме ние?Are we ready?
Той е от Англия.От Англия ли е той?Is he from England?

💡 Word order in ли questions

The focus word moves first: Лекар ли е той? — literally "Doctor is he?" The thing you are asking about (лекар) leads the sentence. Compare: Той ли е лекарят? = "Is he the doctor?" (asking about the person, not the profession). Word order changes meaning in Bulgarian.

Section 5

Real-world scenarios — съм in action

Four situations where the verb to be is your most important tool.

Notice how съм, its negation, and ли questions appear in every one of these everyday exchanges. Listen to each line, then cover the English and try to understand without it.

🏨 Hotel check-in

Настаняване в хотел

At the reception desk. Notice every use of е / сте / ли.

Добър вечер! Имате ли резервация?Dobar vecher! Imate li rezervatsiya?Good evening! Do you have a reservation?
Да. Казвам се Джон Смит. Аз съм от Англия.Da. Kazvam se Dzhon Smit. Az sam ot Angliya.Yes. My name is John Smith. I am from England.
Момент... Да, тук сте. Стаята е на третия етаж.Moment... Da, tuk ste. Stayta e na tretiya etazh.One moment... Yes, you are here. The room is on the third floor.
Включена ли е закуската?Vklyuchena li e zakushtata?Is breakfast included?
Да, закуската е включена. Заповядайте, ключът ви.Da, zakushtata e vklyuchena. Zapovyadayte, klyuchat vi.Yes, breakfast is included. Here is your key.
Много благодаря! Ресторантът включен ли е?Mnogo blagodarya! Restorantat vklyuchen li e?Thank you very much! Is the restaurant included?
Да, ресторантът е отворен от седем до десет.Da, restorantat e otvoren ot sedem do deset.Yes, the restaurant is open from seven to ten.

🏥 Doctor's receptionist

Регистратура при лекар

Registering at a GP surgery. Lots of съм for describing conditions and identity.

Добър ден! Имате ли час при лекар?Dobar den! Imate li chas pri lekar?Good afternoon! Do you have an appointment?
Да. Казвам се Ана Джоунс. Аз съм пациент тук.Da. Kazvam se Ana Dzhoyns. Az sam patsient tuk.Yes. My name is Anna Jones. I am a patient here.
Как се чувствате? Добре ли сте?Kak se chuvstvate? Dobre li ste?How do you feel? Are you well?
Не съм много добре. Имам температура.Ne sam mnogo dobre. Imam temperatura.I am not very well. I have a temperature.
Лекарят е свободен след десет минути.Lekaryt e svoboden sled deset minuti.The doctor is free in ten minutes.
Благодаря. Чакалнята тук ли е?Blagodarya. Chakalnata tuk li e?Thank you. Is the waiting room here?
Да, тя е там вдясно.Da, tya e tam vdyasno.Yes, it is over there on the right.

📞 Phone call — is someone available?

Телефонен разговор

Calling a Bulgarian friend's home. A classic use of е / не е for availability.

Ало?Alo?Hello?
Здравейте! Иван там ли е?Zdraveyte! Ivan tam li e?Hello! Is Ivan there?
Не, той не е вкъщи в момента.Ne, toy ne e vkashti v momenta.No, he is not at home at the moment.
Знаете ли кога ще е там?Znaete li koga shte e tam?Do you know when he will be there?
Той е на работа. Ще е вкъщи след шест.Toy e na rabota. Shte e vkashti sled shest.He is at work. He will be home after six.
Разбирам. Аз съм Том. Може ли да му кажете?Razbiram. Az sam Tom. Mozhe li da mu kazhete?I see. I am Tom. Could you tell him?
Разбира се! Той ще ви се обади.Razbirase! Toy shte vi se obadi.Of course! He will call you back.

☕ Cafe — is it available / included?

Кафене — наличност и включено

Ordering at a Bulgarian cafe and checking what's available. Perfect practice for има ли / включено ли е / свободно ли е.

Добър ден! Свободна ли е тази маса?Dobar den! Svobodna li e tazi masa?Good afternoon! Is this table free?
Да, тя е свободна. Заповядайте!Da, tya e svobodna. Zapovyadayte!Yes, it is free. Please, go ahead!
Включен ли е интернетът тук?Vklyuchen li e internetat tuk?Is the internet included here?
Да, Wi-Fi е безплатен. Паролата е на масата.Da, Wi-Fi e bezplaten. Parolata e na masata.Yes, Wi-Fi is free. The password is on the table.
Кафето с мляко ли е?Kafeto s mlyako li e?Is the coffee with milk?
Не, то е черно. Искате ли мляко?Ne, to e cherno. Iskate li mlyako?No, it is black. Would you like milk?
Да, моля. Включена ли е услугата в сметката?Da, molya. Vklyuchena li e uslugata v smetkata?Yes please. Is service included in the bill?
Не, тя не е включена.Ne, tya ne e vklyuchena.No, it is not included.

Section 6

Writing task

✏️ Writing task — Day 5

  1. Write the full conjugation of съм from memory (all 6 persons, positive form).
  2. Make these sentences negative: "Те са готови."   "Аз съм вкъщи."   "Тя е лекар."
  3. Turn these into ли questions: "Той е лекар."   "Ние сме готови."   "Стаята е свободна."
  4. Translate: "We are not from Bulgaria. Are you from England?" (formal)
  5. Write three sentences about yourself using съм — your job, your nationality, and one thing you are (e.g. ready, tired, married).
Show answers

1. Аз съм · Ти си · Той/Тя/То е · Ние сме · Вие сте · Те са

2. Те не са готови.  |  Аз не съм вкъщи.  |  Тя не е лекар.

3. Лекар ли е той?  |  Готови ли сме ние?  |  Свободна ли е стаята?

4. Ние не сме от България. Вие от Англия ли сте?

5. Example: Аз съм учител. Аз съм от Англия. Аз съм готов/готова.

💡 Tip: Write the six conjugation forms from memory every day this week until they are automatic. Five minutes of active recall beats thirty minutes of passive reading.
