Colours &
Adjectives

Adjectives bring your Bulgarian to life — describing people, things, places and situations. Today you learn the colours, essential descriptive words, and the agreement rule that governs all of them.

📅 Day 10⏳ ~60 minutes🎤 Audio on every section✅ Quiz at the end
🔊 Speed:

🎨 Setting the scene — colour your Bulgarian

You're browsing a fruit market in Plovdiv and want the red apples, not the yellow ones. At the hotel, you need to describe your blue suitcase that went missing. A friend asks what colour car you drive. In every case, the adjective must match the noun — and Bulgarian makes this beautifully systematic. One rule (masculine base, add -а for feminine, -о for neuter, -и for plural) covers virtually every adjective in the language. Today you'll learn it once and use it forever — starting with the colours that fill Bulgarian daily life.

Section 1

Adjective agreement — the one rule behind everything

Adjectives match the gender and number of the noun they describe. Learn this once and it applies to every adjective you will ever use.

In Bulgarian, adjectives must agree with the noun they describe in gender and number. The masculine form is the base; feminine and neuter are made by changing the ending. The rule is very regular — there are almost no exceptions for the endings themselves. Once you know it, you know it for every adjective in the language.

FormEndingнов — newдобър — goodкрасив — beautiful
M Masculine (base)-ен / consonantновдобъркрасив
F Feminine-а / -яновадобракрасива
N Neuter-о / -еноводоброкрасиво
Pl Plural (all genders)-иновидобрикрасиви

Hear all four forms of нов (new)

новnovnew (masculine) — нов стол = new chair
новаnovanew (feminine) — нова книга = new book
новоnovonew (neuter) — ново куче = new dog
новиnovinew (plural) — нови столове = new chairs

💡 The definite article also changes — but only on the first word

When a noun phrase has the definite article ("the"), the article attaches to the first word in the phrase — usually the adjective. Example: новият стол (the new chair — M), новата книга (the new book — F), новото куче (the new dog — N), новите столове (the new chairs — Pl). You do not add articles to both adjective and noun.

Definite phrases — the article on the adjective

новият столnoviyat stolthe new chair (M definite)
новата книгаnovata knigathe new book (F definite)
новото кучеnovoto kuchethe new dog (N definite)
новите столовеnovite stolovethe new chairs (Pl definite)

🌍 Why Bulgarian grammar feels simpler than Russian or Polish

If you've ever glanced at Russian or Polish grammar, you'll know that Slavic adjectives can have six or seven case forms — each with its own ending. Bulgarian threw all of that away. When the language lost its case system (a process completed by the 1600s), adjective agreement simplified dramatically: just four forms (masculine, feminine, neuter, plural) instead of dozens. This makes Bulgarian the easiest Slavic language for adjective agreement — and one reason why linguists sometimes call it "the English of the Slavic world".

Section 4

Comparatives and superlatives — по- and най-

One simple prefix for "more", one for "most". Works with every adjective.

Bulgarian comparatives are beautifully regular: prefix по- (po-) = more / -er, and най- (nay-) = most / -est. These prefixes work with every single adjective in the language — no irregular forms. The agreement endings stay the same: по-голям / по-голяма / по-голямо / по-голями.

💡 Comparisons: по-... от... (more ... than ...)

To say "X is bigger than Y", use: X е по-голям от Y (X is more-big than Y). The word от (from/than) introduces the second element. Example: София е по-голяма от Пловдив. (Sofia is bigger than Plovdiv.)

👔 In the clothes shop

В магазина за дрехи

Watch how colours and adjectives agree with the nouns. Риза (shirt) is feminine, so all descriptors use feminine endings.

Продавач
Добър ден! Мога ли да ви помогна?Dobar den! Moga li da vi pomogna?Good afternoon! Can I help you?

⛅ Talking about the weather

Говорим за времето

Weather is one of the most natural contexts for adjectives. Времето (the weather) is neuter, so adjectives describing it take -о endings.

Иван
Какво е времето днес?Kakvo e vremeto dnes?What is the weather like today?
Анна
Студено и облачно. Небето е тъмносиво.Studeno i oblachno. Nebeto e tamnosivo.Cold and cloudy. The sky is dark grey.
Иван
А утре? Ще е по-топло ли?A utre? Shte e po-toplo li?And tomorrow? Will it be warmer?
Анна
Не, ще е по-студено. Но неделята ще е слънчева и топла.Ne, shte e po-studeno. No nedelyata shte e slancheva i topla.No, it will be colder. But Sunday will be sunny and warm.
Иван
Добре. Ще изляза в неделя тогава — времето е хубаво.Dobre. Shte izlyaza v nedelya togava — vremeto e hubavo.Good. I will go out on Sunday then — the weather is nice.
