This, That
& Pointing

Demonstratives unlock a huge chunk of conversation — describing things nearby, asking what something is, and directing attention. They agree with noun gender, just like articles.

Section 1

This and that — eight forms, one rule

Two sets of four: near (this) and far (that). Each agrees with noun gender and number.

Bulgarian demonstratives work like the definite article from Day 6 — they change depending on the gender and number of the noun they describe. Once you know the noun gender, the right form is automatic. Click every form and say it aloud before moving on.

👉 THIS — near to you (този / тази / това / тези)
Masculineтозиtoziтози мъж — this man
Feminineтазиtaziтази жена — this woman
Neuterтоваtovaтова дете — this child
Plural (all genders)тезиteziтези книги — these books
👆 THAT — far from you (онзи / онази / онова / онези)
Masculineонзиonziонзи хотел — that hotel
Feminineоназиonaziонази улица — that street
Neuterоноваonovaонова кафе — that coffee
Plural (all genders)онезиoneziонези хора — those people

💡 това — the most useful form by far

Това is the neuter singular, but it does far more work than that. Whenever you point at something without knowing its gender, whenever you make a general statement, or whenever you mean "this/that" in isolation — use това: Какво е това? (What is this?)  ·  Това е вярно. (This is true.)  ·  Това е моят приятел. (This is my friend.)

💡 Near vs far — how far is far?

In practice, този/тази/това (this) means something you could point to or touch. онзи/онази/онова (that) means something across the room, at a distance, or something you are gesturing toward with an outstretched arm. The distinction is the same as English this/that — trust your instinct.

Section 3

What is this? — identification questions

The questions every beginner uses constantly. Memorise them all.

These are among the most useful phrases you will ever learn in Bulgarian. As a beginner you will point at things constantly and ask what they are. These five patterns cover almost every situation.

Какво е това?Kakvo e tova?What is this / that? — use when gender is unknown
Как се казва това?Kak se kazva tova?What is this called? — asking for the Bulgarian word
Кой е той?Koy e toy?Who is he? — identifying a man
Коя е тя?Koya e tya?Who is she? — identifying a woman
Това ли е...?Tova li e...?Is this...? — confirming identity
Кой е този / тази / това?Koy e tozi / tazi / tova?Who / what is this? (with known gender)
Какво е онова?Kakvo e onova?What is that (over there)?
Как се казва на български?Kak se kazva na balgarski?What is it called in Bulgarian?

💡 Your most powerful learning tool

Как се казва това? and Как се казва на български? are your most powerful tools as a learner. Point at anything — a sign, a food, an object — and ask a Bulgarian speaker. Every answer gives you a free vocabulary lesson. Use these phrases constantly.

Section 6

Real-world scenarios — this, that and directions in action

Four situations where demonstratives and location words are your main tools.

📍 Finding the hotel in Sofia

Намиране на хотел

Spot every demonstrative and every location word as you read.

Извинете! Хотел Родина тук ли е?Izvinete! Hotel Rodina tuk li e?Excuse me! Is Hotel Rodina here?
Не, не е тук. Онзи хотел е Плиска. Родина е там.Ne, ne e tuk. Onzi hotel e Pliska. Rodina e tam.No, it is not here. That hotel is Pliska. Rodina is there.
В коя посока? Направо ли?V koya posoka? Napravo li?In which direction? Straight on?
Направо и после вляво. Хотелът е срещу парка.Napravo i posle vlyavo. Hotelat e sreshtu parka.Straight on and then left. The hotel is opposite the park.
Далеч ли е? Аз не съм от тук.Dalech li e? Az ne sam ot tuk.Is it far? I am not from here.
Близо е — около пет минути пеш.Blizo e — okolo pet minuti pesh.It is close — about five minutes on foot.
Тази улица ли е правилната?Tazi ulitsa li e pravilnata?Is this the right street?
Да, тази е. Много благодаря!Da, tazi e. Mnogo blagodarya!Yes, this is it. Thank you very much!

🛍 In a shop — this one or that one?

В магазина — това или онова?

Shopping is where demonstratives get used most. Pointing replaces vocabulary you do not yet have.

Добър ден! Мога ли да помогна?Dobar den! Moga li da pomogna?Good afternoon! Can I help?
Да. Какво е това? — тази синя чанта.Da. Kakvo e tova? — tazi sinya chanta.Yes. What is this? — this blue bag.
Това е кожена чанта от България.Tova e kozhena chanta ot Balgariya.This is a leather bag from Bulgaria.
А онази чанта там — каква е?A onazi chanta tam — kakva e?And that bag over there — what is it?
Онази е платнена. Тази е по-хубава.Onazi e platnena. Tazi e po-hubava.That one is fabric. This one is nicer.
Колко струва тази?Kolko struva tazi?How much is this one?
Тази е двадесет евро. Онази е петнадесет.Tazi e dvadeset evro. Onazi e petnadeset.This one is twenty euros. That one is fifteen.
Ще взема тази. Благодаря!Shte vzema tazi. Blagodarya!I will take this one. Thank you!

😰 Lost tourist — is this the right place?

Изгубен турист

Confirming you are in the right place uses това ли е constantly.

Извинете — това ли е улица Витоша?Izvinete — tova li e ulitsa Vitosha?Excuse me — is this Vitosha Street?
Не, тази улица е Граф Игнатиев. Витоша е там.Ne, tazi ulitsa e Graf Ignatiev. Vitosha e tam.No, this street is Graf Ignatiev. Vitosha is there.
Онази улица ли е — вдясно?Onazi ulitsa li e — vdyasno?Is that the street — on the right?
Да, онази е. Направо и после вдясно.Da, onazi e. Napravo i posle vdyasno.Yes, that is it. Straight on and then right.
Това ли е центърът на града?Tova li e tsentarat na grada?Is this the city centre?
Да! Тук е центърът. Народният театър е там, пред вас.Da! Tuk e tsentarat. Narodniyat teatar e tam, pred vas.Yes! The centre is here. The National Theatre is there, in front of you.

✈️ At the airport — which gate?

На летището — коя врата?

Airports are a maze of demonstratives — this terminal, that gate, those screens.

Извинете! Тази врата за Лондон ли е?Izvinete! Tazi vrata za London li e?Excuse me! Is this gate for London?
Не, тази е за Берлин. Лондон е онази врата — там.Ne, tazi e za Berlin. London e onazi vrata — tam.No, this one is for Berlin. London is that gate — there.
Далеч ли е? Трябва ли да вървя?Dalech li e? Tryabva li da varvya?Is it far? Do I need to walk?
Не, близо е. Тези врати са в същия терминал.Ne, blizo e. Tezi vrati sa v sashtiya terminal.No, it is close. These gates are in the same terminal.
Онова табло там — за моя полет ли е?Onova tablo tam — za moya polet li e?That board there — is it for my flight?
Да, онова е общото табло. Вашият полет е там, горе.Da, onova e obshtoto tablo. Vashiyat polet e tam, gore.Yes, that is the main board. Your flight is there, at the top.
Много благодаря! Тук ли е паспортният контрол?Mnogo blagodarya! Tuk li e pasportniyat kontrol?Thank you very much! Is passport control here?
Не, паспортният контрол е там, вляво.Ne, pasportniyat kontrol e tam, vlyavo.No, passport control is there, on the left.

Writing task

✏️ Writing task — Day 7

  1. Write "this man", "this woman", "this child", "these books" in Bulgarian.
  2. Write "that man", "that woman", "that child", "those people" in Bulgarian.
  3. Translate: "What is that over there? — That is the bank. It is opposite the hotel, on the left."
  4. Write the four forms of both "this" and "that" (all 8 forms from memory).
  5. Write three sentences pointing at things near you and three pointing at things far away, using objects from Day 6.
Show answers

1. този мъж, тази жена, това дете, тези книги

2. онзи мъж, онази жена, онова дете, онези хора

3. Какво е онова? — Онова е банката. Тя е срещу хотела, вляво.

4. THIS: този · тази · това · тези   THAT: онзи · онази · онова · онези

5. Example: Тази маса е тук. Тази книга е моя. Това дете е тук. — Онзи стол е там. Онази стая е далеч. Онова кафе е студено.

💡 Tip: Write both complete sets of demonstratives from memory every day this week — THIS: този · тази · това · тези   THAT: онзи · онази · онова · онези. The patterns are symmetrical once you see them.
