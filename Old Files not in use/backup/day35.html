A2 grammar — complete reference

Every grammar point from Days 16–34 in one place.

Grammar pointKey ruleExample
Aorist (simple past)Group 1: stem + -х/-е/-е/-хме/-хте/-ха. Group 2: stem + -их/-и/-и/-ихме/-ихте/-иха. Key irregulars: бях, отидох, дойдох, взех, казах, видях, ядох, пих.Вчера отидох на пазара. Тя каза нещо важно.
Future tenseще + present tense (all persons). Negative: няма да + present. No new endings.Ще отида утре. Няма да работя в събота.
Comparativesпо- + adjective (more). най- + adjective (most). Completely regular — no exceptions.по-евтин · по-голям · най-добър · по-бавно
Reflexive verbsсе = action on oneself (събуждам се, обличам се). си = for oneself (лягам си, мия си зъбите).Събуждам се в шест. Лягам си в единадесет.
Frequency adverbsвинаги · обикновено · често · понякога · рядко · никога (+ не)Никога не закъснявам. Рядко гледам ТВ.
Боли ме constructionболи ме + singular body part. болят ме + plural body part.Боли ме главата. Болят ме зъбите.
Profession genderEvery Bulgarian profession has M and F forms. F usually adds -ка/-ица.лекар/лекарка · учител/учителка · програмист/програмистка
Работя катоProfession after като/съм: bare noun, NO definite article.Работя като инженер. (not инженерът)
Честит agreementЧестит (M) · Честита (F) · Честито (N) — agrees with noun.Честит рожден ден! · Честита сватба! · Честито бебе!
Seasons + презчерез + definite season noun: през пролетта/лятото/есента/зимата.През лятото е горещо. През зимата вали сняг.
Object pronouns (intro)го (M/N) · я (F): Ще го взема (M noun). Ще я взема (F noun).Ще го купя (панталона). Ще я взема (ризата).
Opinion phrasesМисля, че · Според мен · Смятам, че · Убеден съм, че · Струва ми сеСпоред мен е важно. Струва ми се, че греши.

Section 3

Bulgarian citizenship exam — complete guide

Everything you need to know before sitting it.

The Bulgarian citizenship exam (изпит по български език и гражданска интеграция) is required for naturalisation under Article 12 of the Bulgarian Citizenship Act. Here is exactly what to expect.

AspectDetails
Who must take itAnyone applying for Bulgarian citizenship by naturalisation who has not completed education in Bulgarian.
Format20 multiple-choice questions. 4 options each. 60 minutes.
Pass mark60% — you need at least 12 correct answers out of 20.
Language level testedApproximately A2 — you are now at this level.
Topics testedBulgarian language (grammar, vocabulary, reading), Bulgarian history, national holidays, geography, culture, symbols, famous Bulgarians.
Where takenOrganised by the State Agency for Bulgarians Abroad and the Ministry of Justice. Usually held in Sofia; other locations possible.
CostFee applies — check current rates with the Ministry of Justice.
RetakesYou may retake if you fail. Check current rules for waiting period.
Preparation tipDays 1–35 of this course cover everything tested. Day 31 (culture) and Day 9 (days/dates) are especially important.

🏆 What this course covers vs the exam

The citizenship exam tests: (1) basic Bulgarian comprehension and vocabulary — covered in Days 1–20; (2) grammar (present, past, question forms) — Days 5, 12, 13, 23; (3) Bulgarian history and national holidays — Day 31; (4) culture and traditions — Day 31; (5) geography and famous Bulgarians — Day 31. Days 1–35 of this course cover every topic area tested. If you can pass the Day 15 mock and Day 35 mock, you are ready.

💡 Your B1 reset checklist before Day 36

Before you start Day 36, make sure you can: (1) Conjugate regular verbs in present AND aorist — both groups. (2) Form the future with ще and the negative with няма да. If any of these feel shaky, revisit the relevant day before moving on.
