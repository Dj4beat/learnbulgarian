Section 1

This and that — the four forms

One set for near (this), one for far (that), all agreeing with gender.

Bulgarian demonstratives agree with the gender and number of the noun they refer to — just as you learned with articles on Day 6. Once you know the gender of a noun, choosing the right form is automatic.

MasculineFeminineNeuterPlural
This (near)този (tozi)тази (tazi)това (tova)тези (tezi)
That (far)онзи (onzi)онази (onazi)онова (onova)онези (onezi)
🎤 This and that

💡 Това — the most useful form

Това (this/that — neuter) is by far the most used demonstrative in Bulgarian. Whenever you are pointing at something without knowing its gender, or making a general statement, use това: Какво е това? (What is this?) · Това е вярно. (This/That is true.) It works like English "this/that" used in isolation.

Section 2

What is this? — the most useful question

Three ways to ask about unknown things.

BulgarianRomanisedEnglishWhen to use
Какво е това?Kakvo e tova?What is this/that?Pointing at something unknown — gender irrelevant
Кой е той?Koy e toy?Who is he?Asking about a male person
Коя е тя?Koya e tya?Who is she?Asking about a female person
Как се казва това?Kak se kazva tova?What is this called?Asking the name/word for something
Това ли е...?Tova li e...?Is this...?Confirming identification

Section 5

Dialogue — finding the hotel

A complete directions exchange using this and that.

💬 Dialogue — asking for directionsRead aloud

— Извинете! Хотел "Родина" тук ли е?
— Не, не е тук. Онзи хотел е "Плиска". "Родина" е там — направо и вляво.
— Далеч ли е?
— Не, близо е. Около пет минути пеш.
— Тази улица ли е правилната?
— Да, тази е. Хотелът е срещу парка.
— Много благодаря!

— Izvinete! Hotel Rodina tuk li e?
— Ne, ne e tuk. Onzi hotel e Pliska. Rodina e tam — napravo i vlyavo.
— Dalech li e?
— Ne, blizo e. Okolo pet minuti pesh.
— Tazi ulitsa li e pravilnata?
— Da, tazi e. Hotelat e sreshtu parka.
— Mnogo blagodarya!

— Excuse me! Is Hotel Rodina here?
— No, it's not here. That hotel is Pliska. Rodina is there — straight ahead and left.
— Is it far?
— No, it's close. About five minutes on foot.
— Is this the right street?
— Yes, this is it. The hotel is opposite the park.
— Thank you very much!
