Negation &

Saying no and asking questions are two of the most fundamental things you do in any language. Today you master both — including the Bulgarian double negative that surprises English speakers.

Section 1

Simple negation with не

One word does everything — and it always goes directly before the verb.

Не (ne) is placed directly before any verb to negate it. Unlike English, you never need "do/does/did" in Bulgarian negation — just put не before the verb. That is the whole rule.

PositiveNegativeEnglish
Говоря български.Не говоря български.I speak / I don't speak Bulgarian.
Той разбира.Той не разбира.He understands / doesn't understand.
Имаме кола.Нямаме кола.We have / don't have a car. (имам → нямам)
Тя е вкъщи.Тя не е вкъщи.She is / isn't home.
Отивам на работа.Не отивам на работа.I'm going / not going to work.
🎤 Simple negation

💡 Имам is special

Имам (have) has its own negative verb: нямам. You do NOT say не имам — this is wrong. Always use нямам. Similarly, има (there is) becomes няма (there is no).

Section 2

Bulgarian double negatives — correct grammar

In Bulgarian, two negatives are required, not forbidden.

This surprises English speakers: in Bulgarian, when you use a negative pronoun or adverb (никой, нищо, никога, никъде), you must also put не before the verb. Leaving out не sounds wrong. This is standard educated Bulgarian — not an error.

BulgarianRomanisedEnglish (literal → real meaning)
Никой не идва.Nikoy ne idva."Nobody doesn't come" → Nobody is coming.
Нищо не знам.Nishto ne znam."Nothing I don't know" → I know nothing.
Никога не закъснявам.Nikoga ne zakasnavam."Never I don't arrive late" → I never arrive late.
Никъде не отивам.Nikade ne otivam."Nowhere I don't go" → I'm not going anywhere.
Нито едното, нито другото.Nito ednoto, nito drugoto.Neither one nor the other.

Section 3

Yes/No questions with ли

Revisiting the ли particle with full verbs.

You learned ли on Day 5 with съм. The same rule applies to all verbs: place ли directly after the verb (or after the focus element you want to highlight). The word order is otherwise unchanged.

Statementли questionEnglish question
Говориш български.Говориш ли български?Do you speak Bulgarian?
Той чете вестника.Вестника ли чете той?Is it the newspaper he's reading?
Имате кола.Имате ли кола?Do you have a car?
Тя работи тук.Тук ли работи тя?Is it here that she works?

All eight question words in action.

QuestionRomanisedExample answer

🎤 Question words in use

💡 Защото vs защо

Защо? = Why? (question word) · Защото... = Because... (connective). These two always appear as a pair. You will use them constantly — memorise both together.

Section 5

Dialogue — answering and asking

Negation and questions in a realistic exchange.

💬 Dialogue — finding out about someoneRead aloud

— Говориш ли английски?
— Да, говоря. А ти говориш ли български?
— Малко. Не разбирам всичко още.
— Защо учиш български?
— Защото жена ми е българка.
— Никога ли не е живяла в Англия?
— Не, никога. Живее тук от раждането си.

— Govorish li angliyski?
— Da, govorya. A ti govorish li balgarski?
— Malko. Ne razbiram vsichko oshte.
— Zashto uchish balgarski?
— Zashtoto zhena mi e balgarka.
— Nikoga li ne e zhivyala v Angliya?
— Ne, nikoga. Zhivee tuk ot razhdaneto si.

— Do you speak English?
— Yes, I do. And do you speak Bulgarian?
— A little. I don't understand everything yet.
— Why are you learning Bulgarian?
— Because my wife is Bulgarian.
— Has she never lived in England?
— No, never. She has lived here since birth.

Section 6

Writing task

✏️ Writing task — Day 13

  1. Make these sentences negative: "Говоря руски." and "Имам кола."
  2. Write three sentences using никой, нищо, and никога.
  3. Translate: "Why are you learning Bulgarian? Because I love Bulgaria and I want to live there."
  4. Form ли questions from: "Тя работи в София." and "Имате свободна стая."
Show answers

1. Не говоря руски. Нямам кола. (note: нямам, not не имам)

2. E.g. Никой не знае. Нищо не разбирам. Никога не закъснявам.

3. Защо учиш български? Защото обичам България и искам да живея там.

4. Работи ли тя в София? Имате ли свободна стая?

💡 Tip: Writing by hand in Cyrillic is the fastest way to lock in new vocabulary. Even five minutes of handwriting beats reading the same words ten times.
